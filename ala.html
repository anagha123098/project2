ALAPPUZHA

ALAPPUZHA – THE VENICE OF THE EAST

Alappuzha (also known as Alleppey) is Kerala’s iconic backwater paradise, often called the “Venice of the East.” Crisscrossed with canals, lagoons, lakes, and paddy fields, this district is a must-visit for houseboat cruises, traditional village life, and the spectacular Nehru Trophy Boat Race. Whether it’s a romantic escape or a relaxing vacation, Alappuzha offers a unique charm that blends nature and culture beautifully.

Tourist Attractions

  • Vembanad Lake – The longest lake in India and heart of the backwaters; ideal for houseboats and sunset views.
  • Alleppey Beach – A calm, scenic beach with a historic pier extending into the sea.
  • Krishnapuram Palace – A 17th-century palace with mural paintings, architecture, and antique collections.
  • Pathiramanal Island – A small, lush island on the lake, perfect for birdwatching and nature walks.

Unique Experiences

  • Stay overnight in a traditional kettuvallam (houseboat) with meals served on board.
  • Witness the thrilling Nehru Trophy Boat Race held every August.
  • Try a Shikara ride through narrow canals for a close-up view of village life.
  • Watch toddy tapping and try fresh local toddy in nearby villages.

Foods to Try

  • Karimeen Pollichathu (Pearl Spot fish wrapped in banana leaf)
  • Chemmeen Curry (spicy prawn curry)
  • Kappa (Tapioca) with Fish
  • Duck Roast – A special delicacy in the Kuttanad region
