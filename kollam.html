KOLLAM

KOLLAM – THE CASHEW CAPITAL AND BACKWATER BEAUTY

Kollam, one of Kerala’s oldest seaport towns, is a treasure trove of history, backwaters, beaches, and cashew. Once a major trading hub with ancient civilizations like Rome and China, the district still retains its heritage charm. Today, Kollam is known for its scenic Ashtamudi Lake, vibrant boat rides, colonial architecture, and being a gateway to Kerala’s famous backwaters.

Tourist Attractions

  • Ashtamudi Lake – The second-largest lake in Kerala, ideal for houseboat cruises and birdwatching..
  • Thangassery Lighthouse – A 144-foot tall colonial lighthouse offering a panoramic view of the Arabian Sea.
  • Jatayu Earth’s Center – Home to the world’s largest bird sculpture, based on the mythical eagle Jatayu from the Ramayana.
  • Palaruvi Waterfalls – A beautiful 300-foot waterfall located in a lush forest setting.
  • Rameshwara Temple – An ancient Shiva temple known for its sculptures and historical significance.

Unique Experiences

  • Take a luxury houseboat cruise on Ashtamudi Lake, especially at sunset.
  • Visit a cashew processing factory to see how the world-famous Kollam cashews are made.
  • Explore Munroe Island – a hidden gem of narrow canals, canoe rides, and village life.
  • Attend the Kollam Pooram festival – vibrant processions, fireworks, and caparisoned elephants.

Foods to Try

  • Cashew Chicken or Cashew Curry
  • Karimeen (Pearl Spot) Pollichathu – grilled fish in banana leaf
  • Tapioca with spicy fish curry
  • Traditional Sadhya at a local vegetarian restaurant
