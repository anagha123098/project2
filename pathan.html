PATHANAMTHITTA

PATHANAMTHITTA – THE PILGRIM'S PARADISE

Pathanamthitta is often referred to as the “Pilgrim Capital of Kerala”, owing to the world-famous Sabarimala Temple that attracts millions of devotees every year. But beyond spirituality, it’s a land blessed with rivers, dense forests, rubber plantations, and untouched natural beauty. This district is perfect for spiritual journeys, offbeat eco-tourism, and exploring rural Kerala.

Tourist Attractions

  • Sabarimala Temple – One of the largest annual pilgrimages in the world, dedicated to Lord Ayyappa, nestled in the Western Ghats.
  • Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple – A temple with architectural beauty and spiritual significance, also known for the Aranmula Boat Race.
  • Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls – A scenic spot perfect for picnics and photography.
  • Kaviyoor Rock Temple – An ancient rock-cut cave temple from the 8th century, dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Konni Elephant Training Center – A unique opportunity to interact with elephants and learn about their care..

Unique Experiences

  • Witness the Aranmula Vallamkali (snake boat race) – a major cultural event during Onam.
  • Explore traditional craftsmanship of the Aranmula Kannadi – a handcrafted metal mirror found nowhere else in the world.
  • Take a river cruise on the Pamba River, surrounded by thick greenery.
  • Visit Adavi for bamboo rafting through serene forest streams.

Foods to Try

  • Appam with Stew
  • Boiled Tapioca with fish curry
  • Ela Ada – steamed rice cakes with coconut and jaggery filling
  • Local vegetarian Sadhya during temple festivals
